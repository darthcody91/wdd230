Design Principles

White Space

Star Wars

starwars.com

White space or "Negative space" refers to the space between different elements such as images and text. While the color doesn't have to be a literal white color, on the Star Wars website, they do a great job using the empty space, as seen above. In fact, they designed the image to "bleed" into the white. This effect gives the user a sense of smoothness between the picture and text.

Visual Hierarchy

Hoonigan

hoonigan.com

There are many things that can define what visual hierarchy is. Typically, most people associate it with the size of elements...but color can also help them be more prominent. Many sites use a combination of both, especially those selling merchandise. As shown on the image, they do a great job using color and size to get the user's eye to move around. The "select options" is a big red box that stands out against the white background. The different weight of the typeface also plays a part in creating eye movement with visual hierarchy.

Hick's Law

Xbox | Microsoft

xbox.com

Hick's Law means that with more choices available, the time to make a decision increases. Picking between two things is much easier than a couple hundred. Using filters helps eliminate the choices. Like in the image above of the Xbox store, there are 2587 games to pick from. Impossible for a user to spend time and look through every single game...if not boring. By giving a filter and a method of sorting, users looking for a racing game can narrow it down from over 2000 games to 170. Thus, making it easier for the user to find what they want, and ultimately making a purchase.
