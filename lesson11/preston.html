People of Preston

This week in People of Preston, we want to spotlight Ms. Golda June. Golda was born and raised in Preston. She was born in 1930 in the potato shed on the farm. He married her late husband, Davey June in 1950. They had nine children together, have 40 grandchildren and 10 great-grandchildren. She loves to cook, knit, play canasta, read the paper, and spend time with the kids. She loves to have the kids over to show them the farm.

Her oldest son, Steve has taken over the potato farm. She enjoys making anything with potatos, and selling them on Saturday's at the farmer's market. Keep watch for her booth, and her "tater salad".

Golda has decided to share her famous tater salad with her community.

GOLDA'S TATER SALAD

  • 5 Potatos
  • 3 Eggs
  • 1 Cup chopped celery
  • 1/2 Cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 Cup sweet pickle relish
  • 1/4 Teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1/4 Teaspoon celery salt
  • 1 Tablespoon perepared mustard
  • Ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 Cup mayonnaise
  • 1 cup of grandma's love

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add potatoes and cook until tender but still firm, about 15 minutes. Drain, cool, peel and chop.

Place eggs in a saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring water to a boil; cover, remove from heat, and let eggs stand in hot water for 10 to 12 minutes. Remove from hot water, cool, peel and chop.

In a large bowl, combine the potatoes, eggs, celery, onion, relish, garlic salt, celery salt, mustard, pepper and mayonnaise. Mix together well and refrigerate until chilled.
